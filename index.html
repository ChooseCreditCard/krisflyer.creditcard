Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer: Miles, Status & Credit Cards

KrisFlyer is the loyalty program of Singapore Airlines and a key Star Alliance ecosystem. This guide explains how KrisFlyer miles are earned, how status tiers work, what typical redemptions look like and how credit cards and transfer partners help you build a strong mileage balance.

Best Cards for Earning KrisFlyer Miles

1. What the KrisFlyer Program Is

KrisFlyer is Singapore Airlines’ frequent-flyer program. Members earn miles on Singapore Airlines, Scoot and Star Alliance partners and can redeem them for flights and upgrades across a wide global network.

  • A mileage currency – KrisFlyer miles used for flights, upgrades and some partner rewards.
  • Elite status tiers – KrisFlyer Elite Silver/Gold and PPS Club tiers.
  • Credit card and transfer links – many bank points can be converted into KrisFlyer miles.

2. How KrisFlyer Miles Are Earned

You can earn KrisFlyer miles via:

  • Singapore Airlines & Scoot flights – miles based on booking class and distance.
  • Star Alliance partners – credit eligible flights to KrisFlyer instead of other programs.
  • Credit cards – either direct KrisFlyer co-branded cards or bank points that transfer to KrisFlyer.
  • Hotel, car rental and lifestyle partners – where you add your KrisFlyer number or convert points.
  • Promotions – occasional bonus miles for specific routes, cabins or partners.

In many markets, the bulk of miles comes from bank reward points that are periodically transferred into KrisFlyer rather than from flights alone.

3. How Credit Cards Boost KrisFlyer Earnings

Credit cards are one of the most powerful tools for building KrisFlyer balances, especially in Asia-Pacific and markets where bank points transfer to multiple airlines. Typical features include:

  • Miles-per-dollar equivalents on everyday spend after conversion.
  • Bonus categories such as dining, travel or online shopping.
  • Welcome bonuses that can be enough for one or more regional flights.
  • Transfer flexibility – some bank points can go to KrisFlyer or competing airlines.

A good strategy is to earn flexible bank points first, then convert them into KrisFlyer when you see award seats you actually want.

4. Redemption Categories in KrisFlyer

KrisFlyer miles can be redeemed for:

  • Singapore Airlines flights – including economy, premium economy, business and Suites (on select aircraft).
  • Star Alliance partner flights – across a global partner network.
  • Upgrades from economy to higher cabins on eligible fares.
  • Spontaneous Escapes – periodic discounted award tickets released by Singapore Airlines.
  • Other partner rewards (e.g. hotels or experiences in some markets).

Many enthusiasts focus on business and first-class awards, where KrisFlyer miles can unlock high-value redemptions compared with the cash price of tickets.

5. KrisFlyer Elite Status & PPS Club

KrisFlyer has multiple recognition levels, broadly split into KrisFlyer Elite tiers and PPS Club tiers:

  • KrisFlyer – base member tier.
  • KrisFlyer Elite Silver – entry elite level with selected perks.
  • KrisFlyer Elite Gold – stronger benefits and Star Alliance Gold recognition.
  • PPS Club – revenue-based tier for high spenders in premium cabins.
  • Solitaire PPS – top-tier recognition for the highest-spending customers.

While credit cards generally do not grant full elite status in KrisFlyer itself, they can help you fly more often in higher cabins, making qualification easier if you track your spending and routing.
